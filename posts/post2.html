The Hidden World of Insects

Insects make up more than half of all known living organisms. Their adaptability and sheer numbers allow them to thrive in nearly every habitat on Earth.

Importance to Ecosystems

Insects play crucial roles, such as pollinating plants, decomposing organic matter, and being a source of food for many animals.

Unique Adaptations

Some insects have evolved to blend into their surroundings, while others use bright colors to ward off predators. Their survival strategies are as diverse as their species.
